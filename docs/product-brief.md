# Product brief

## Summary

Generation Patient needs a public, interactive 2025 annual report that translates a document into an editorial web experience without losing the convenience of print and PDF. The report should communicate impact, deepen trust, and make dense policy and community work approachable.

## Primary audiences

- Young adults living with chronic or rare conditions
- Current and prospective donors
- Patient advocates and partner organizations
- Policymakers, researchers, clinicians, and journalists
- Generation Patient staff maintaining the report

## Product goals

1. Present the 2025 story as one cohesive, scroll-based narrative.
2. Let one non-technical administrator update copy and replace images in Sanity.
3. Provide both a polished browser print experience and a downloadable PDF.
4. Generate the website and PDF from the same published content.
5. Use restrained interaction to clarify impact metrics and relationships in the story.
6. Preserve Generation Patient's independent, patient-led voice.

## Non-goals

- A general-purpose page builder
- A live analytics dashboard
- External live-data integrations
- Video or rich-media management
- Marketing analytics or behavioral tracking
- A custom editorial approval workflow
- A reusable multi-year report platform in the first release
- Formal accessibility certification

## Experience direction

### Web

- One story-driven page with clear chapters and shareable section anchors
- Strong opening statement and high-level impact metrics
- Progressive movement from overview to direct support, systems change, community stories, future priorities, and donors
- Editorial interactions such as animated metrics or explorable relationships, used only where they strengthen comprehension
- Persistent or contextual chapter navigation without turning the report into a dashboard

### Print and PDF

- A dedicated print treatment using the same content as the website
- Deliberate page breaks, print-safe colors, and static alternatives to interactions
- Browser Print / Save as PDF support
- A matching downloadable PDF regenerated after each published deployment

### Editing

- One Sanity administrator
- Fixed fields for each approved report section
- Copy editing and image replacement only
- Controlled image placement, with no editor-defined layout system
- Draft preview before publishing

## Content outline

The supplied copy currently implies this narrative:

1. Cover
2. 2025 at a Glance
3. Key results in 2025
4. Thank you
5. Looking Forward
6. Our Work in Depth
7. Peer Support
8. Health Policy Lab and four policy priorities
9. Community Spotlight: Peyton Miles
10. Roundtables and global consensus
11. CCYAN
12. Policy participation and media coverage
13. Our Donors

The final sequence may be refined during design, but content must not be omitted or materially rewritten without editorial approval.

## Visual direction

The official Generation Patient identity is the foundation:

- Navy: `#011329`
- Green family led by `#A9DE34`
- Cyan family led by `#41EBFD`
- Official Generation Patient logo and wordmark

The annual report may extend the core identity using the R0 drafts' oversized typography, rotation, asymmetric composition, outlined geometry, cutout photography, and high-contrast editorial pacing. The R0 layouts are directional, not exact production specifications.

The brand sheet identifies Mont/Mont Bold and Poppins. The R0 drafts explore additional expressive display typography. Final font licensing, availability, and legibility must be resolved before implementation.

## Success criteria

- An editor can update approved copy and replace images without changing code.
- Published web and PDF versions do not drift.
- The report remains coherent on desktop, mobile, browser print, and generated PDF.
- Interactive content adds understanding and degrades to a clear static print view.
- No unsupported facts or invented data enter the experience.
- The finished design feels recognizably Generation Patient while exceeding the visual quality of a document conversion.

## Deferred decisions

- Whether later reports reuse this architecture
- Exact interactive treatments and datasets
- Final section-level design and motion
- Font selection and licensing
- Production domain
- PDF generation implementation
- Hosting and CMS account configuration
